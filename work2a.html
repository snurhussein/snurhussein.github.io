Social Network Analysis

Nalewa Mada, Kanuri for “we are looking for peace,” began out of necessity to respond to a growing and complex security and humanitarian crisis in Niger’s Diffa region. In an effort to enhance the resiliency of communities against Boko Haram, USAID-OTI’s Niger Community Cohesion Initiative (NCCI) brought together 12 community leaders to create a committee, Nalewa Mada, to share security information and develop the leaders’, and as a consequence the communities’, capacity to prevent and respond to violent extremism. That effort grew, and Nalewa Mada now serves as the leading body of 300 community leaders, across 30 village committees, which are working together to respond to security threats. Using social network analysis, the dynamics and functioning of the Nalewa Mada network can be explored. Interactive visuals available on kumu.
