Duékoué youth lead dialogue on common interests among divided communities

CITI provided a grant to support a city-wide dialogue about what the Duékoué cultural center means to its residents and how they can contribute to its rebirth. 93 youth from 12 neighborhoods and three villages participated in the youth-led community mapping exercise, developing and administering a questionnaire to nearly 2,000 Duékoué residents. After tabulating and analyzing the results, the youths presented the findings to a gathering of their peers, community elders, and city authorities. One participant commented that “the elders and other members of our communities reached out to us to praise us for our work. It feels like we matter to our communities now, that we are part of the rebuilding process and that is a good feeling,”
